### What is Coherent Breathing?

Coherent Breathing is a simple yet powerful breathing technique that involves breathing at approximately 5 breaths per minute — roughly 6 seconds per breath (5-6 seconds inhale, 5-6 seconds exhale).

This technique, developed by Stephen Elliott in 2005 and featured in James Nestor's bestselling book "Breath," has been scientifically shown to:

- ✅ Reduce stress and anxiety
- ✅ Improve heart rate variability (HRV)
- ✅ Enhance mental focus and clarity
- ✅ Promote better sleep
- ✅ Balance the autonomic nervous system
- ✅ Increase feelings of calm and well-being

### Step-by-Step: How to Practice Coherent Breathing

**Step 1: Find a Comfortable Position**
- Sit upright in a chair or lie down
- Place one hand on your chest, one on your belly
- Relax your shoulders and jaw

**Step 2: Breathe Through Your Nose (If Possible)**
- Inhale slowly for 5-6 seconds
- Feel your belly rise (not your chest)
- Exhale slowly for 5-6 seconds
- Let your belly fall naturally

**Step 3: Maintain the Rhythm**
- Keep the ratio close to 1:1 (inhale = exhale)
- Don't hold your breath
- Let the breath flow naturally

**Step 4: Practice for 10-20 Minutes**
- Start with 10 minutes daily
- Work up to 20 minutes
- Best times: Morning, before meals, or before bed

✓ No air hunger (feeling like you need more air)

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Breathing too fast (aim for 5 breaths/min)  
❌ Using your chest instead of your belly  
❌ Holding your breath between inhale/exhale  
❌ Forcing the breath — let it flow naturally  
❌ Practicing on a full stomach (wait 1-2 hours)
